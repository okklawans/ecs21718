Yoko Ono

Ono

Yoko Ono is an artist that was born on February 18, 1933.

Ono was married to John Lennon before he sadly died on Decemeber 8, 1980.

She was an anti-war activist and created many songs that there to be peace around the entire world, and she rallied for the war to come to an end.

5 Achievements

  • Ono recieved the Grammy Award for Album of the Year in 1982 for 'Double Fantasy' along with Jack Douglas and John Lennon.
  • During the Vietnam War, Yoko Ono and John Lennons held multiple "Bed-Ins For Peace" which were non-violent protests against the war. This has influences many protests over time.
  • In 2016, she won the NME Inspiration Award for her work in the arts and activism.
  • Yoko Ono married John Lennon on March 20, 1969.
  • Around 1945, Ono moved to New York City and attended Sarah Lawrence College.
